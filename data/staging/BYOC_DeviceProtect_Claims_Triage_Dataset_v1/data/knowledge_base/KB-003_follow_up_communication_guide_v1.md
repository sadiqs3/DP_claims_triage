# Follow-up Communication Guide

**Document ID:** KB-003  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective date:** 2026-06-23  
**Purpose:** Define safe, concise, and neutral communication when more information is required for claims triage.

## 1. Communication objective

A follow-up message should obtain the minimum information needed to continue assessment. It should not accuse, pressure, promise an outcome, or ask the customer to interpret policy rules.

## 2. Required style

Use language that is:

- Specific: identify the missing fact or document.
- Neutral: state what is needed without implying fault.
- Actionable: explain how the customer can provide it.
- Bounded: ask only for information needed for the current decision step.
- Non-committal: do not state or imply final approval, final rejection, payment amount, repair authorization, or fraud concern.

## 3. Approved response structure

1. Acknowledge the claim submission.
2. State the missing or unclear item.
3. Explain why it is needed in plain language.
4. Give a clear next action.
5. State that assessment will continue after receipt, without promising a result.

## 4. Examples

### Missing incident date
“Thanks for submitting your claim. Please confirm the date on which the incident occurred. We need this date to continue assessing the claim under the policy period.”

### Missing device identifier
“Please provide the IMEI or serial number for the device involved in the claim. This helps us confirm the covered-device details.”

### Ambiguous incident category
“Please describe what happened to the device and the issue you are experiencing, such as a cracked screen, liquid exposure, a mechanical malfunction, or another type of damage.”

### Missing required evidence
“Please upload a clear copy of the requested document or image. Once it is received, the claim assessment can continue.”

### Unreadable upload
“The submitted file could not be read clearly. Please upload a complete and legible copy, ensuring all relevant pages or details are visible.”

## 5. Do not use

- “Your claim will be approved once you send this.”
- “You are not covered unless you prove otherwise.”
- “We believe this may be fraudulent.”
- “You must provide every document you have.”
- “The policy definitely does / does not cover this” unless the rule engine has produced the verified outcome and the communication is being reviewed through the approved downstream process.

## 6. Escalation instead of follow-up

Do not ask the customer to resolve internal data conflicts, duplicate policy records, or a source-system discrepancy. Route those cases to manual review with the applicable reason code.
